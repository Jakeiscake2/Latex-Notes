\noindent \\Definition
\\We identify a dedekind cut with a real number.
\\More precisely, $\alpha:=A|A'$ is called a real number. 
\\If A' has a minimum, $\alpha$ is called rational.
\\If A' does not have a minimum, $\alpha$ is called irrational.
\\\\Remark: We will still use the same symbols to denote both a rational number and the dedekind cut defining it.
\\Example:
\\$\frac{1}{2}$ = $\frac{1}2$ and A|A' where $A=\{x\in Q|x<\frac{1}2\}$ and $A'=\{x\in Q|x\geq \frac{1}2\}$
\\\\The set of all real numbers is denoted as $\mathbb{R}$

\\Definition:
\\Let $\leq$ be a binary relation on $\mathbb{R}$, st $\forall a=A|A', b=B|B'\in \mathbb{R}$
\\$a\leq b:=A\subseteq B$
\\$a=b:=A=B$
\\$a<b:=A\subsetneq B$

\noindent\\Proposition: $\leq$ is a total order
\\Proof:
\\Reflexivity: $\forall \alpha = A|A' \in R, A\subseteq A\implies \alpha \leq \alpha$
\\Anti-Symmetry:
\\$\forall a=A|A', b=B|B'\in R$
\\$a\leq b\wedge b\leq a\implies A\subseteq B\wedge B\subseteq A\implies A=B\implies a=b$
\\Transitivity:
\\$\forall a=A|A',b=B|B',c=C|C'\in R$
\\$a\leq b\wedge b\leq c\implies A\subseteq B\wedge B\subseteq C\implies A\subseteq C\implies a\leq c$
\\Totality:
\\$\forall a=A|A',b=B|B'\in R$
\\If $a\leq b$, we are done
\\Then $A\not \subseteq B$, thus there exists an element $y\in A,y\not \in B$.
\\$a\not \leq b\implies A\not \subseteq B\implies \exists y\in Q, y\in A, y\not \in B \implies y\in B'$
\\Now $\forall x\in A',\forall z\in B$
\\$x>y>z\implies x\in B'\implies A'\subseteq B'\implies B\subseteq A$
\\Thus, if $a\not \leq b$, then $b\leq a$
