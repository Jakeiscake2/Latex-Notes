\subsection{Local Properties}
\noindent Proposition: Let $f:I_\delta(x)\to \mathbb R$ be continuous at $ x_0$.
\\Then,
\begin{enumerate}
    \item (Local Boundedness)
    \[\exists \delta>0,\exists M\geq 0: \forall x\in I_\delta (x_0),|f(x)\leq M|\]
    \item (Local Sign Definteness)
    \[\text{If }f(x_0)>0,\text{then }\exists \delta>0:\forall x\in I_\delta(x_0)\]
    \end{enumerate}
\noindent Proof:
\\Take any $\epsilon>0$, there exists $\delta>0 $ such that 
\[\forall x\in I_\delta(x_0), |f(x)-f(x_0)|<\epsilon\]
\[|f(x)|<\epsilon+|f(x_0)|\]
Take $\epsilon=f(x_0)/2$, then exists $f>0$ such that 
\[\forall x\in I_\delta(x_0),|f(x)-f(x_0)|<\frac{f(x_0)}{2}\implies f(x)>f(x_0)-f(x_0)/2=f(x_0)/2>0\]

\\Let $A\subseteq \mathbb R$, we say $f:A\to \mathbb R$ is continuous in A if $\forall x\in A$, f is continuous at x. We denote this by by $f\in C(A)$
\subsection{Example}
\noindent $C((a,b))=\{f:(a,b)\to \mathbb R|\text{ f is continuous at each (a,b)}\}$
\\$C((a,b))=\{f:[a,b]\to \mathbb R|\text{ f is continuous at each (a,b)}\}$
\\Note that a closed interval will have no left limit at a and no right limit at b.
\\a is the left limit of f, b is the right limit of f.
\subsection{Theorem}

By local sign definiteness, the set is not empty.\\
